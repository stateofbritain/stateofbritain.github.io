### 2. Effective scrutiny — Chris Bryant (Lab)

**Debate:** Processed Russian Oil Products: Sanctions
**Time:** 2026-05-20T13:00:31

**Why it matters.** The Minister for Trade, Chris Bryant, apologised at the despatch box for the Government's handling of new licences covering fuel refined from Russian crude.

> “The one bit of the right hon. Gentleman's remarks that I completely agree with is that we have handled this clumsily. That is entirely my fault, and I apologise to all hon. Members”

The urgent question, granted in response to the Conservative MP Andrew Bowie, concerned trade licences permitting imports of diesel and jet fuel refined from Russian crude. ### 14. Reframe — Sir Julian Lewis (Con, New Forest East)

**Debate:** Defence Readiness
**Time:** 2026-05-20T15:09:59

**Why it matters.** Julian Lewis (CON), a former Defence Committee chair, argued that peacetime defence spending is cheap insurance, since a full-scale war would force spending to 30% or 40% of GDP.

> “If, by investing in defence in peacetime, we can deter a potential enemy from starting a war, we not only save the treasure; we save the blood that is otherwise shed so copiously.”

Lewis argued that a full-scale war with a peer adversary would force defence spending to 30% or 40% of GDP, not 3% or 4%, so peacetime investment is the cheaper course. He compared it to pandemic preparedness, where capability is funded only once the crisis arrives.
