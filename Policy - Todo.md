### Data
- [ ] Add appeal statistics - how many deportation decisions are overturned on appeal, broken down by Article 3 vs Article 8 if possible
- [ ] Add a one-line note to the grant rate table linking high grant rates to Article 3 countries (Afghanistan 98% = the same countries where deportation is blocked)
- [ ] Surface the binary nature of grant rates - the system has two modes: near-automatic approval for Article 3 countries (Afghanistan 98%, Eritrea 93%, Sudan 88%) and near-automatic refusal for non-Article 3 countries (Albania 3%, India, Vietnam). Very little middle ground. The framework, not caseworker discretion, determines most outcomes. This also reframes the backlog: a large share of pending cases are from Article 3 countries where the outcome is near-certain but still takes 18 months to process. The bottleneck is capacity, not decision difficulty
